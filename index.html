Rishabh Agrawal

I am currently a PhD student at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering and an M.S. in Computer Science student at University of Southern California, Los Angeles.

My research, under the supervision of Prof. Rahul Jain and Prof. Ashutosh Nayyar, focuses on reinforcement learning, with a particular emphasis on imitation learning and foundation models.

Previously, I worked as a Research Engineer at Samsung, where I applied deep reinforcement learning to address network-related challenges. During my undergraduate years, I worked with Prof. Sriparna Bandopadhyay at Indian Institue of Technology Guwahati on data augmentation using r-cyclic matrices. During Summer '18, I worked as an intern under the supervision of Prof. Richard James at University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, on mathematical modeling of light-induced phase transition. During Summer'17, I worked as an intern under the supervision of Prof. Frank Chung-Hoon Rhee at Hanyang University, on estimating the fuzzifier parameter for alpha planes of General Type-2 Fuzzy Sets.
