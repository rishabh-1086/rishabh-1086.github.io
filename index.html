Rishabh Agrawal

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Southern California (USC), Los Angeles. I completed Masterâ€™s programs in both Computer Science and Electrical Engineering at USC in May 2025.

My research, under the supervision of Prof. Rahul Jain and Prof. Ashutosh Nayyar, focuses on reinforcement learning, with a particular emphasis on imitation learning and foundation models.

Previously, I worked as a Research Engineer at Samsung, where I applied deep reinforcement learning to address network-related challenges. During my undergraduate years, I worked with Prof. Sriparna Bandopadhyay at Indian Institue of Technology Guwahati on data augmentation using r-cyclic matrices. In Summer 2018, I interned under Prof. Richard James at University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, working on the mathematical modeling of light-induced phase transitions. In Summer 2017, I worked as an intern under the supervision of Prof. Frank Chung-Hoon Rhee at Hanyang University, focusing on estimating the fuzzifier parameter for alpha planes of General Type-2 Fuzzy Sets.
